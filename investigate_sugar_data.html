    Conclusions¶

  • Back to Table of Contents

    • In conclusion, the global trend for sugar intake steadily increased over the 10 year period from 1995 to 2004. Our analysis has highlighted that females have a tendency to have higher BMIs than males. We have also seen an interesting positive correlation between sugar intake and health costs per person. The positve correlation between BMI and sugar consumption is not as strong but exists. The world is getting unhealthier, as we see that there are 20% less countries whose population's average weight was deemed as healthy in 2004 compared with 1995. Samoa was the world's most obese population in 2004. The United States has the world's highest sugar consumption rate, while the highest health costs per person are attributed to Norway. It should be noted however that the cost of living in Norway is approx. 30% higher* than in the United States and is the 6th most expensive country in the world to live in.

    *Source: https://www.numbeo.com/cost-of-living/compare_countries_result.jsp?country1=Norway&country2=United+States
